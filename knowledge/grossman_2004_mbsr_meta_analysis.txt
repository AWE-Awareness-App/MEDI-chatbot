Title: Grossman 2004 – Mindfulness-Based Stress Reduction (Meta-Analysis)

Type: Meta-analysis
Population: Mixed medical and non-medical populations
Intervention: MBSR (8-week structured program)
Year: 2004

Topic Tags:
- mindfulness
- mbsr
- anxiety
- depression
- stress
- quality_of_life
- meta_analysis

----------------------------------------

OVERVIEW

This meta-analysis examined the overall effectiveness of Mindfulness-Based Stress Reduction (MBSR) across clinical and non-clinical populations.

The analysis included studies evaluating psychological distress, anxiety, depression, and overall wellbeing.

----------------------------------------

KEY FINDINGS

Across studies, MBSR showed:

✔ Moderate reductions in stress
✔ Moderate reductions in anxiety
✔ Improvements in depressive symptoms
✔ Improvements in overall psychological wellbeing

Effects were consistent across different populations, including individuals with chronic illness.

----------------------------------------

PROGRAM STRUCTURE

Typical MBSR program:

- Duration: 8 weeks
- Weekly sessions: ~2–2.5 hours
- Practices:
  - Body scan
  - Sitting meditation
  - Mindful movement / gentle yoga
  - Daily home practice

----------------------------------------

INTERPRETATION

The findings suggest that structured mindfulness programs can meaningfully improve psychological wellbeing across diverse groups.

----------------------------------------

MEDI-SAFE LANGUAGE

• “Research shows structured mindfulness programs can reduce stress and anxiety.”
• “Regular meditation practice has been linked to improved emotional balance.”

----------------------------------------

IMPORTANT BOUNDARY

This evidence supports stress reduction and emotional support.
It does not replace psychiatric or medical treatment.

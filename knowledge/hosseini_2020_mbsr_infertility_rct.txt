Title: Hosseini 2020 – MBSR for Women with Infertility (Randomized Controlled Trial)

Type: Randomized Controlled Trial
Population: Women with infertility
Intervention: 8-week MBSR
Year: 2020

Topic Tags:
- infertility
- anxiety
- depression
- quality_of_life
- mindfulness
- rct

----------------------------------------

OVERVIEW

This randomized controlled trial evaluated the effect of an 8-week MBSR program on quality of life in women with infertility.

----------------------------------------

KEY FINDINGS

Compared to routine consultation, MBSR significantly improved:

✔ Overall quality of life
✔ Mental health
✔ Emotional functioning
✔ Vitality
✔ General health perception

No significant change observed in:
- Social functioning
- Bodily pain

----------------------------------------

INTERPRETATION

Short-term MBSR can significantly improve psychological wellbeing in women coping with infertility.

----------------------------------------

PROGRAM STRUCTURE

- 8 weekly sessions (2 hours each)
- Body scan
- Breathing meditation
- Gentle yoga
- Home practice

----------------------------------------

MEDI-SAFE LANGUAGE

• “Studies suggest mindfulness programs can improve emotional wellbeing for women coping with infertility.”
• “Mindfulness may help manage stress related to treatment.”

----------------------------------------

IMPORTANT BOUNDARY

Mindfulness supports emotional coping but does not affect fertility outcomes directly.

Title: Khoury 2015 – Mindfulness-Based Therapy Meta-Analysis

Type: Large meta-analysis
Population: Clinical and non-clinical adults
Intervention: MBSR, MBCT, and related mindfulness therapies
Year: 2015

Topic Tags:
- mindfulness
- mbsr
- mbct
- anxiety
- depression
- mental_health
- meta_analysis

----------------------------------------

OVERVIEW

This comprehensive meta-analysis evaluated mindfulness-based therapies across a large number of studies.

----------------------------------------

KEY FINDINGS

Mindfulness-based interventions demonstrated:

✔ Moderate effect sizes for anxiety reduction
✔ Moderate effect sizes for depression reduction
✔ Improvements in stress and emotional regulation
✔ Comparable effectiveness to other established therapies for some conditions

Mindfulness was particularly effective for anxiety disorders and mood-related symptoms.

----------------------------------------

INTERPRETATION

Mindfulness-based therapies are evidence-supported psychological interventions for improving emotional health.

----------------------------------------

PROGRAM STRUCTURE

Most interventions:
- 8-week format
- Group-based sessions
- Formal meditation + informal daily awareness practices

----------------------------------------

MEDI-SAFE LANGUAGE

• “Mindfulness programs are supported by research for reducing anxiety and depressive symptoms.”
• “These practices can strengthen emotional resilience.”

----------------------------------------

IMPORTANT BOUNDARY

Mindfulness supports mental health but does not replace professional psychiatric care.

Title: Ledesma 2008 – MBSR in Cancer Patients (Meta-Analysis)

Type: Meta-analysis
Population: Cancer patients
Intervention: Mindfulness-Based Stress Reduction
Year: 2008

Topic Tags:
- cancer
- anxiety
- depression
- stress
- quality_of_life
- mindfulness
- meta_analysis

----------------------------------------

OVERVIEW

This meta-analysis evaluated the effects of MBSR on psychological outcomes in cancer patients.

----------------------------------------

KEY FINDINGS

✔ Moderate improvement in mental health outcomes
✔ Reduced anxiety and depression
✔ Reduced stress
✔ Improved mood and sleep quality
✔ Smaller effects on physical symptoms

Mental health improvements were stronger than physiological effects.

----------------------------------------

INTERPRETATION

Mindfulness appears particularly helpful for emotional coping during cancer treatment.

----------------------------------------

PROGRAM STRUCTURE

- 8-week structured MBSR program
- Group sessions
- Meditation, body scan, mindful movement

----------------------------------------

MEDI-SAFE LANGUAGE

• “Research shows mindfulness programs can help people facing cancer manage stress and emotional distress.”
• “Mindfulness may improve quality of life during treatment.”

----------------------------------------

IMPORTANT BOUNDARY

Mindfulness supports emotional wellbeing but is not a cancer treatment.
